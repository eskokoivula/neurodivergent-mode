# Foundation: neurodivergent imagination as a reasoning engine

This document is the theoretical basis for the `neurodivergent-mode` skill. Read it
when you need to deepen a reframe beyond the operational instructions in `SKILL.md`,
or when someone asks why the method is built the way it is.

One rule governs this document: **every concept is written out in plain language.**
A citation is a footprint — it shows where an idea comes from and lets a reader
check it. It never replaces the explanation. If a concept is only named, it is not
really here. So each idea below is opened fully: what it is, how it works, what to
do with it. The references are listed at the end.

---

## 1. The core: deliberate mind-wandering

The engine in `SKILL.md` has one core motion: **drift, then return.** Wander away
from the problem on purpose, then come back on purpose. Both halves are mandatory.

This is not a metaphor. It is taken directly from research on how creative thinking
actually works.

Mind-wandering — the mind moving off the task it is on — comes in two kinds, and
the difference is everything:

- **Spontaneous mind-wandering** is losing the thread. Attention drifts on its own,
  without intent, and does not reliably come back. It is uncontrolled.
- **Deliberate mind-wandering** is letting attention move off-task *on purpose*, and
  steering it back. It is controlled.

Research on attention and creativity finds that only the *deliberate* kind is linked
to higher creative ability. Drifting alone does not produce creative work; drifting
with intent and return does. (Footprint: Fang and colleagues' work presented at
ECNP 2025 on mind-wandering type and creativity in ADHD; see References.)

This single distinction is the spine of the whole skill:

- **Drift** is the deliberate move off the obvious path — into detail, into other
  domains, into other people's experience.
- **Return** is the deliberate move back — building a principle, testing it, landing
  a usable result.
- A skill that only drifts produces a jumpy transcript. A skill that never drifts
  produces a worksheet. The method needs both, in that order, on purpose.

This is reinforced by a second, independent line of research. Creativity is widely
modelled as **two alternating phases**: a *generation* phase (producing ideas, wide
association) and an *evaluation* phase (judging, monitoring, selecting). The two-fold
model describes a *cyclic motion* between them — ideas are generated, then common or
weak ones are rejected while novel and fitting ones get further attention and
elaboration. The evaluation phase is split into three sub-stages: valuation,
monitoring, selection. (Footprint: Kleinmintz, Ivancovsky & Shamay-Tsoory 2019.)

Two research lines, reached independently, point at the same shape: generation and
evaluation must alternate. That is drift and return. The engine is built on it.

## 2. The four drift directions

Drift is not random. The mind wanders in specific directions, and each one is a
documented feature of neurodivergent cognition. The skill uses four. Each is opened
below as an operation — what it does, not just what it is called.

### 2.1 Detail-first

**What it is.** Start from the raw, concrete details and anomalies of the problem.
Do not impose a frame, summary, or conclusion before the details have been examined.
Let the general pattern be built upward, out of the specifics — not handed down from
an assumption.

**Why it works.** In autistic cognition, perception of local detail tends to be
primary. Two research models describe this:

- The *enhanced perceptual functioning* model holds that lower-level perceptual
  processing — detecting, discriminating, and categorising fine detail — is
  heightened, and that higher-level, big-picture processing is *optional* rather than
  *mandatory*. In typical cognition the global view is automatic; in autistic
  cognition the local view is the default and the global view is switched on
  deliberately. (Footprint: Mottron and colleagues, 2006.)
- The *weak central coherence* account describes the same detail-focused style from
  the other side. Note honestly: this theory is contested — some studies do not
  replicate it, and people can process globally when explicitly asked to. The skill
  therefore uses the careful version: local detail is the *default*, global is a
  *deliberate switch* — not "global processing is broken." (Footprint: Happé & Frith
  and the weak central coherence literature.)

There is also a documented *drive to build systems* from detail — to take a set of
specifics, find the rules connecting them, and construct a predictable model.
Measured as the "systemizing quotient," this drive is on average markedly stronger
in autistic adults. (Footprint: Baron-Cohen and colleagues, 2003. Note: that paper
also advances the "extreme male brain" theory, which is contested; the skill uses
only the measured systemizing finding, not that wider framing.)

**What detail-first does in the engine.** It is the opening move of drift: list the
concrete anomalies, the things that do not fit the tidy story, *before* any framing.
Then build a rule-system upward from them.

### 2.2 Divergent

**What it is.** Reach for far, unobvious associations — deliberately look for the
same structure in unrelated domains: biology, history, software, markets, music.
Generate widely before narrowing.

**Why it works.** Divergent thinking — fluency, flexibility, and originality in
producing many varied responses to an open question — is associated with ADHD
traits. A population study found that more ADHD traits predicted higher divergent
thinking scores. Two honest limits: the link is not uniform across all studies, and
it plateaus — beyond a certain level of symptoms it stops rising and can fall. So
this is a real tendency, not a superpower. (Footprint: Stolte and colleagues, 2022.)

**What divergent does in the engine.** It is the deliberate off-road part of drift:
the structured wander into other fields, looking for a transferable pattern.

### 2.3 Social

**What it is.** Hold several interpretations of a situation side by side. Do not
assume one "normal" reading. Ask explicitly whose experience differs and why.

**Why it works.** The old model said autistic people have a one-directional deficit
in understanding others' minds. Current research revises this in two ways:

- A meta-analysis of theory-of-mind studies in autistic adults found that differences
  are *task-specific* — they depend on what is being measured — rather than an
  all-or-nothing absence. Theory of mind in autism is atypically developed and
  context-dependent, not simply missing. (Footprint: Gao, Wang & Su 2023.)
- The *double empathy* idea reframes the breakdown as *mutual*. When two people are
  disposed very differently, misunderstanding runs both ways — it is not one side's
  defect. Experimental work supports this: in rated video conversations, mixed
  autistic / non-autistic pairs were judged least successful, and non-autistic
  observers underestimated autistic participants relative to how their actual
  conversation partners rated them. The mismatch is relational, not a one-sided
  failing. (Footprint: Milton 2012; Jones and colleagues, 2024.)

**What social does in the engine.** It forces the reframe to ask whose experience of
the problem differs — and to treat a breakdown between groups as a two-way mismatch,
not one group's deficiency.

### 2.4 Global — the deliberate switch (return-side)

Global is not a fourth drift direction. It belongs to *return*, and it is best
understood as a switch.

The enhanced perceptual functioning research gives the reason: the big-picture view
is *optional* in this cognitive style, activated on purpose rather than running
automatically. So in the engine, "zoom out to the whole system" is a deliberate act
performed at the return — not a background default. The skill states the switch
openly: *now zoom out.*

## 3. The return

Drift gathers. Return makes something usable. Without return, drift is just a
transcript.

The two-fold model of creativity gives return its internal shape: the evaluation
phase has three sub-stages — **valuation** (judging which raw material is worth
keeping), **monitoring** (checking the emerging idea against the problem), and
**selection** (committing to the principle and the moves that follow). The skill's
return runs in that order: build the principle, test it, then produce the
generative leap and challenge the default frame. (Footprint: Kleinmintz, Ivancovsky
& Shamay-Tsoory 2019.)

Return also includes the deliberate global switch (Section 2.4) and the use of
internal simulation — mentally rehearsing how a system, idea, or design behaves
before committing to it. This is the cognitive habit visible in the historical
figures below, and it has a clean conceptual basis in the philosophy of the thought
experiment: turning a problem into a mental scene and examining it from the inside.
(Footprint: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Thought Experiment.")

The return described here runs *inside* the engine. The skill takes it one step
further: it pulls the evaluation phase out of the engine and runs it as a separate
adversarial pass — the selection layer, Section 4.

## 4. The selection layer — evaluation made separate

The engine in Sections 1–3 generates a reframe. A reframe is a hypothesis: it can
be bold and right, or bold and wrong — and "doubt the frame, trust the anomaly" is
also how every crank thinks. The selection layer is the part that tells them apart.
It is the *evaluation phase* of the two-fold model (Section 1), pulled out and made
adversarial, run as a deliberate pass *after* the engine rather than woven through
it.

Two separations carry the layer, and each has a reason.

**Separate in time: generate first, judge second.** The two-fold model is *cyclic* —
generation and evaluation alternate, they do not run at once (Footprint: Kleinmintz,
Ivancovsky & Shamay-Tsoory 2019, Section 1). When evaluation runs *inside*
generation, the valuation sub-stage rejects raw material before it is elaborated —
and the first thing it rejects is the unfamiliar, because unfamiliar is what looks
weak before it is built out. Holding judgment until the reframe is fully built is
not politeness to the idea; it is what protects the novelty the skill exists to
find. This is the project's core wager, one level down: *separation protects
creativity.*

**Separate in mind: a different evaluator, not the same one grading itself.** This
is an engineering choice, not a research finding, and is marked as such. The
reasoning: a self-critic re-applies its own frame, so it shares its own blind spots
— and a skill built to escape default frames cannot make the frame-holder the
judge. Where the environment can spawn a second agent, the critic runs as a
separate mind blind to the engine's reasoning; in plain chat, the same model runs a
strict separate pass. Either way the point is distance from the thing being judged.

**What a kill may rest on — and the one thing it may not.** A reframe may be killed
only on (1) logic — a contradiction is false in any paradigm — or (2) a real test
the critic runs: a calculation, a primary source, a named counterexample. It may
*not* be killed on (3) consensus. Consensus can only *flag*. The reason is
structural: every paradigm shift was false by the consensus of its era, and this
skill exists precisely to beat consensus, so consensus cannot be its judge without
killing the thing it is meant to find. Consensus is a strong prior, never a verdict.
(This is the same discipline the "Citations and research" section of `SKILL.md`
applies to factual claims: a kill is load-bearing, so it must be sourced, not
asserted from memory.)

## 5. Why the failure modes happen

`SKILL.md` bans six anti-patterns, in two sets. The first three are failures of the
engine — of deliberate mind-wandering itself. The second three are failures of the
selection layer that follows it. None is arbitrary; each is a specific mechanism,
and naming it is what lets the skill recognise and avoid it.

**Of the engine — drift and return.**

- **The worksheet.** Drift suppressed. The method's steps become visible headings and
  labelled lists; the form eats the content. This is what happens when return runs
  without real drift ever happening — structure with nothing wandered-for inside it.
- **The stream.** Spontaneous mind-wandering leaked onto the page. Ideas are stated,
  gestured at, jumped away from, with no return. This is the *spontaneous* kind of
  wandering from Section 1 — drift with the return missing.
- **The clever fragment.** A sharp reframe with no developed body. The mind drifted,
  found something genuinely good, and then stopped — return began but never finished.
  Valuation happened; monitoring and selection did not.

These three are the same lesson: deliberate mind-wandering is drift *and* return,
both complete. Remove either half and you get one of them.

**Of the selection layer — evaluation gone wrong.**

- **The conformity filter.** The critic kills an idea for being weird, not wrong —
  judging the destination's strangeness instead of testing the road. This is
  consensus smuggled back in as a verdict, and it leaves exactly the obvious answer
  the engine worked to escape.
- **The pre-censored generator.** Separation in time fails: the generator softens
  its ideas in anticipation of the critic, so evaluation has leaked into generation
  and novelty is lost before the judge ever runs.
- **The lossy kill.** A refuted idea is dropped in silence instead of named with its
  reason. A critic that hides its kills cannot be told from a careless one.

These three are the evaluation phase run wrong: judging too early, judging the wrong
target, or judging without leaving a trace.

## 6. Historical models — illustration, not foundation

Einstein, Tesla, Grandin, and da Vinci appear in this project as **illustrations** of
internal-simulation thinking — not as its evidence base. The distinction matters and
is kept deliberately. This section opens one of them fully, because an abstract
phrase like "he used thought experiments" teaches nothing — the *steps* are the
point.

### 6.1 Worked example: how Einstein's thinking reaches a result

Take the thought experiment Einstein is most associated with: imagining what it
would be like to chase a beam of light and travel alongside it. Followed step by
step, it is a clean illustration of drift → return — the same motion `SKILL.md`
runs.

1. **Start from a concrete detail, not a frame (drift · detail-first).** Einstein
   does not begin with "what is wrong with physics." He begins with one specific,
   physical image: a beam of light, and an observer moving next to it at the same
   speed. The problem is made concrete and small before any theory is touched.

2. **Build the scene and run it internally (drift · internal simulation).** He does
   not calculate yet. He puts himself *inside* the scene and asks what the observer
   would actually see. Running it: travelling alongside the beam at its own speed,
   the light should appear frozen — a stationary wave hanging in space.

3. **Detect the contradiction (the hinge).** That frozen wave is the anomaly. It
   contradicts what the physics of the time allowed: light's behaviour was not
   supposed to produce a standing, motionless wave like that. The simulation has
   surfaced something the accepted frame cannot hold. This is the detail that does
   not fit — found by running the scene, not by arguing about theory.

4. **Doubt the frame, not the detail (return · challenge the default).** Faced with
   the contradiction, the move is not "the image must be wrong." It is: the *frame*
   must be wrong. Specifically, the unexamined assumption — that time runs the same
   for every observer — is the thing that breaks. The anomaly is trusted; the
   convention is doubted.

5. **Build the new principle (return · selection).** Dropping the assumption forces
   a new one in its place: the speed of light is constant for every observer, and
   time itself must vary instead. The reframe is not decoration on the old physics —
   it is the only structure left standing once the contradiction is taken seriously.

The shape is exact: a concrete detail → a scene run from the inside → an anomaly the
frame cannot hold → doubt the frame, not the detail → a rebuilt principle. That is
drift then return. The historical example is here to make that motion visible — not
to prove it. (The method's evidence is in Sections 1–3; the conceptual basis for
"run a scene from inside" is the philosophy of the thought experiment — Footprint:
Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.)

This description is given in the project's own words. It does not put quotation
marks around anything attributed to Einstein, because the skill does not invent or
reconstruct quotations for real people.

### 6.2 The others, briefly

- **Tesla** described mentally building, adjusting, and testing inventions before
  construction, refining until he saw no fault — an imagine / simulate / detect-error
  / revise loop.
- **Da Vinci's** notebooks show relentless observation converting into principle — a
  bottom-up path to insight.
- **Temple Grandin** describes her own thinking as picture-based and similar to
  mentally test-running designs before building them.

What these show is *what internal simulation looks like in practice*. What they do
**not** do is establish how neurodivergent cognition works — none of them is a
controlled study, and retrospective diagnosis of historical figures is speculation.
The mechanism comes from the research in Sections 1–3. These figures only make it
concrete. Keep them in that role.

## 7. Important caution

Do not turn neurodivergence into a single superpower story. The research does not
support one, and this project explicitly refuses to imply one.

The honest picture: the creative profile in autism is, on average, *below* control
groups on standard creativity measures — lower fluency and flexibility — though with
higher detail and higher originality (Footprint: Pennisi 2020, meta-analysis). The
ADHD / divergent-thinking link plateaus and is not uniform (Stolte 2022). The
mind-wandering / creativity finding is recent and not yet replicated (Fang, ECNP
2025). More traits is not better.

The accurate model is not "better thinking." It is **different optimisation**: more
detail salience, more internal simulation, more pattern-based association, sometimes
less automatic acceptance of convention — coexisting in real life with overload,
communication friction, and uneven attention. This skill deliberately takes the
cognitive *structure* useful for reasoning and applies it as a method. It does not
claim that this is how all neurodivergent people think, or that thinking this way
makes anyone neurodivergent. That restraint is what makes it a tool rather than a
caricature.
